About Asteroid Belt

The asteroid belt is the circumstellar disc in the Solar System located roughly between the orbits of the planets Mars and Jupiter. It is occupied by numerous irregularly shaped bodies called asteroids or minor planets. Read more

Facts about It

The asteroid belt contains hundreds of thousands of known asteroids, while there are millions and maybe billions which are still unknown.

Gravitational influences can move asteroids out of the belt, often sending them into the outer solar system.

Despite the many objects in the asteroid belt, the space is so vast and the distance between objects so large (around 600,000 miles) than space craft are able to fly through the belt without colliding with any asteroids. More Facts
